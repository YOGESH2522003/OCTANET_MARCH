28-Day Diet and workout Plan

Week 1 - Foundation and Endurance



Day 1: Full Body

1.Squats: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
2.Bench Press: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
3.Deadlifts: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
4.Plank: 3 sets of 30-60 seconds

Day 2: Cardio & Core

1.30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio (e.g., jogging, cycling)
2.Russian Twists: 3 sets of 15 reps per side
3.Leg Raises: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
Day 3: Rest or Light Activity

Day 4: Upper Body

1.Pull-Ups: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
2.Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
3.Bicep Curls: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
4.Tricep Dips: 3 sets of 12-15 reps

Day 5: Lower Body

1.Lunges: 3 sets of 12-15 reps per leg
2.Leg Press: 3 sets of 12-15 reps
3.Calf Raises: 3 sets of 15-20 reps
4.Hamstring Curls: 3 sets of 12-15 reps

Day 6: Cardio & Agility

1.30 minutes of high-intensity interval training (HIIT)
2.Agility Ladder Drills: 15 minutes

Day 7: Rest

Weeks 2 to 4 - Progression and Intensity

A.Full Body & Upper Body Days:

1.Increase the weight used by 5-10% if the last set of an exercise feels manageable.
2.Introduce variations like incline bench press instead of the regular bench press, or try dumbbell exercises for added stabilization.

B.Lower Body Day:

1.Consider incorporating more compound movements like barbell squats and deadlift variations (e.g., sumo, Romanian). Increase sets or reps gradually.

C.Cardio & Core Days:

1.Add more time or intensity to your cardio sessions.
2.Introduce new core exercises like planks with leg lifts, side planks, or medicine ball exercises.

D.Rest and Recovery:

1.Continue to allow at least one full day of rest.
2.Consider incorporating active recovery or light activity on rest days to promote mobility and flexibility, such as yoga or a gentle walk.
3.Remember, proper form and technique are crucial to prevent injury and achieve the best results. Additionally, listening to your body and adjusting the intensity of workouts based on how you're feeling is essential. Hydration, nutrition, and adequate sleep play significant roles in recovery and performance.

Diet for all weeks [Non-veg diet]-



1.Breakfast Options:

A.Oatmeal and Fruit:
A bowl of oatmeal topped with a handful of berries (blueberries, strawberries) and a spoonful of chia seeds. This meal is rich in fiber, antioxidants, and omega-3 fatty acids.
B.Egg White Veggie Omelette:
An omelette made with egg whites, spinach, tomatoes, and mushrooms. Serve with a slice of whole-grain toast. This provides a lean protein source and fiber.
C.Greek Yogurt and Nuts:
A serving of low-fat Greek yogurt with a small handful of almonds and slices of banana. This combination offers protein, healthy fats, and potassium.

2.Lunch Options:

A.Grilled Chicken Salad:
A salad with mixed greens, grilled chicken breast, avocado, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and a vinaigrette dressing. This meal is balanced with lean protein, healthy fats, and fiber.
B.Quinoa and Roasted Vegetables:
A bowl of quinoa with roasted broccoli, carrots, and bell peppers, topped with a drizzle of olive oil and lemon juice. Quinoa is a complete protein and provides essential amino acids.
C.Turkey Wrap:
A whole-grain wrap filled with lean turkey slices, lettuce, tomatoes, and hummus. Pair with a side of carrot sticks for extra fiber.

3.Dinner Options:

A.Salmon and Asparagus:
A grilled salmon fillet served with steamed asparagus and a side of quinoa or brown rice. This meal is rich in omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and protein.
B.Vegetable Stir-Fry with Tofu:
A stir-fry with tofu, broccoli, bell peppers, snap peas, and carrots in a light soy sauce. Serve over brown rice. This meal provides a good mix of protein, fiber, and vitamins.
C.Chicken and Sweet Potato:
Oven-baked chicken breast with a side of roasted sweet potato and green beans. This offers a good balance of protein, complex carbs, and fiber.

*General Tips*:

1.Stay hydrated throughout the day by drinking plenty of water.
2.Limit added sugars and refined grains.
3.Adjust portion sizes according to your specific calorie needs and activity level.
4.Consider incorporating physical activity into your routine for better weight management.

Diet for all weeks [Veg diet]-



1.Breakfast Options:

A.Poha (Flattened Rice):
Light and easy to digest, poha is a popular breakfast choice. It's typically prepared with onions, mustard seeds, turmeric, and sometimes peas or peanuts for added protein. You can add a handful of chopped vegetables like carrots and green beans to increase its fiber content.
B.Upma:
Made from semolina (rava), upma can be enriched with vegetables like carrots, peas, and capsicum. It's seasoned with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies, offering a flavorful start to the day.
C.Mid-Morning Snack:
Fruit: A mid-morning fruit snack, such as an apple or a bowl of papaya, can provide a healthy fiber and vitamin boost.

2.Lunch Options:

A.Bhakri (Jowar or Bajra Roti) with Zunka:
Bhakri is a gluten-free flatbread, and zunka is a spicy gram flour preparation. This combination is not only filling but also high in protein and fiber.
B.Mixed Sprout Usal:
A mix of sprouted legumes cooked with traditional Maharashtrian spices, providing a high-protein and nutritious addition to your meal.
C.Koshimbir (Salad):
A simple cucumber, tomato, and onion salad with a lemon juice dressing can add freshness and fiber to your meal.

3.Dinner Options:

A.Varan Bhat (Dal Rice):
A simple preparation of lentil curry served with steamed rice. It's comforting, easy to digest, and provides a good balance of carbohydrates and protein.
B.Bharli Vangi (Stuffed Brinjal):
Small brinjals stuffed with a spicy coconut and peanut masala, offering flavorsome and nutritious addition to dinner.
C.Chapati:
Having one or two chapatis made from whole wheat flour can be a healthier alternative to rice if preferred.

*General Tips*:

1.Stay hydrated throughout the day by drinking plenty of water.
2.Limit added sugars and refined grains.
3.Adjust portion sizes according to your specific calorie needs and activity level.
4.Consider incorporating physical activity into your routine for better weight management.
